Reunite — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 11, 2026

The short version

Reunite helps families plan reunions. You join with an invite code — no account, no password. We collect only what's needed to run the event, we don't sell it, and we don't track you across other apps.

What we collect

When you use Reunite, you may provide:

  • Your name — so the organizer and family know who's coming.
  • Your RSVP (Yes / Maybe / No).
  • Optional contact info you choose to add: email, phone number, and mailing address.

If you create a reunion, we also store the reunion details you enter: name, date, location, agenda, and notes for guests.

Automatically, we assign your device an anonymous identifier when the app first opens (an anonymous sign-in). It lets the app remember your RSVP and the reunions you've joined. It is not tied to your real-world identity, your Apple ID, your contacts, or any advertising profile.

We do not collect your location, photos, contacts, or usage analytics, and we do not use third-party trackers or advertising.

To protect against someone guessing invite codes, our servers may temporarily log the network (IP) address of invite-code look-ups. These logs are deleted within one day and are not used for anything else.

How we use it

Only to run the reunion: show the organizer and guests who's coming, let the organizer plan, and let you update your own details. That's it.

Who can see your information

  • Other members of the same reunion can see attendee names and RSVP counts.
  • The organizer of a reunion can see and export the guest list (names and RSVPs) for planning.
  • Your optional email/phone/address are stored on your guest record; they are not shown to other guests in the app.

We use Supabase (our backend hosting provider) to store this data securely. We don't sell or rent your information to anyone, and we don't share it for advertising.

Keeping and deleting your information

  • You can remove your own information any time with "Leave reunion" in the app — it deletes your RSVP and contact info for that reunion.
  • An organizer can delete a whole reunion, which removes its guest list, agenda, and notes.
  • Deleting and reinstalling the app resets your anonymous identifier; your previous data isn't recoverable to the new install.

To request deletion or ask a question, contact us at reunite@evlallc.com.

Children

Reunite is intended for adults organizing and responding to family events. It is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect their information.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update this page and the "last updated" date above.
